EUKEN Nurse Bridge

Connecting qualified Kenyan nurses & caregivers with Germany and Austria
Executive summary: This platform outlines official application authorities, minimum requirements, and realistic timeframes for recognition and recruitment of qualified nurses/caregivers from Kenya to Germany and Austria. We support recruitment partners with end‑to‑end services: document prep, translation, recognition, employer liaison, and visa.

Important note: Germany and Austria do not issue blanket or automatic recognition of foreign institutions or qualifications. Recognition is carried out on an individual, case‑by‑case basis by the competent authority. The institutions and qualifications listed are commonly submitted and assessed — but final decisions rest with each country’s authority.

Germany
Official authorities
  • Anerkennung in Deutschland – central portal www.anerkennung-in-deutschland.de
  • ZAV (Zentrale Auslands- und Fachvermittlung) – supports international recruitment www.arbeitsagentur.de/zav
  • Make-it-in-Germany – official information for skilled workers www.make-it-in-germany.com
Minimum requirements
Nursing diploma & transcripts Curriculum / training hours CV & references Passport copy Police clearance Medical certificate German B1–B2* Certified German translations

*depending on role

Typical timeframes
Recognition processing: approx. 3–6 months (once complete). Additional time if adaptation training/exams are required.
Austria
Official authorities
  • Federal Ministry of Social Affairs, Health, Care (BMSGPK) – recognition of regulated professions www.sozialministerium.at
  • GOEG (Nursing in Austria) – central guidance for recognition/nostrification www.goeg.at
  • AMS (Arbeitsmarktservice) – Austrian Public Employment Service www.ams.at
Minimum requirements
Nursing/caregiver diploma & transcripts Detailed curriculum CV & references Police clearance Medical fitness German B2 (proof) Certified translations
Typical timeframes
Standard recognition: approx. 3–4 months. Longer if adaptation training needed. Shortened one‑stop procedures may apply.

Assessment criteria in Germany & Austria

Recognition authorities compare training duration, theoretical content, clinical practice hours, and supervised placements against national curricula. Outcomes may include:

Full recognition Partial recognition + adaptation training Recognition at assistant level Refusal if substantial differences
Agent workflow (summary)
1. Pre-screen & collect docs
2. Translations & language prep
3. Submit recognition applications
4. Coordinate with ZAV/AMS & employers
5. Visa & relocation support

📌 Compliance statement

All candidates must provide original certificates, official transcripts, verification of institutional accreditation at the time of training, and certified translations. Final recognition decisions rest solely with the competent authority in the destination country.
